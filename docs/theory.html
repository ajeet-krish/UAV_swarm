Theory & Mathematical Formulation

1. Quadcopter State-Space Dynamics

Each agent is modeled as a double-integrator with aerodynamic drag, linearized around a steady hover trim condition. The state vector tracks 3D position and velocity:

$$ \mathbf{x}_i = \begin{bmatrix} x_i & y_i & z_i & \dot{x}_i & \dot{y}_i & \dot{z}_i \end{bmatrix}^T \in \mathbb{R}^6 $$

The continuous-time state-space dynamics are:

$$ \dot{\mathbf{x}}_i = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}_i + \mathbf{B}\mathbf{u}_i + \mathbf{d}_i(t) $$
Eq. 1: Linear Time-Invariant Dynamics
$$ \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{0}_{3 \times 3} & \mathbf{I}_{3 \times 3} \\ \mathbf{0}_{3 \times 3} & -\frac{b}{m} \mathbf{I}_{3 \times 3} \end{bmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{B} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{0}_{3 \times 3} \\ \frac{1}{m} \mathbf{I}_{3 \times 3} \end{bmatrix} $$
Eq. 2: System and Input Matrices

Where \(m = 1.2\) kg is the vehicle mass, \(b = 0.15\) Ns/m is the aerodynamic drag coefficient, \(\mathbf{u}_i \in \mathbb{R}^3\) is the commanded thrust vector, and \(\mathbf{d}_i(t)\) represents wind gust perturbations.

2. Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR)

Rather than tuning individual PID loops, the LQR framework provides optimal state feedback gains by minimizing a quadratic cost function:

$$ J = \int_0^{\infty} \left( \mathbf{x}_e^T \mathbf{Q} \mathbf{x}_e + \mathbf{u}^T \mathbf{R} \mathbf{u} \right) dt $$
Eq. 3: LQR Cost Function

where \(\mathbf{x}_e = \mathbf{x}_i - \mathbf{x}_{\text{target}}\) is the state error. The weighting matrices prioritize position accuracy over control effort:

$$ \mathbf{Q} = \text{diag}(10, 10, 10, 1, 1, 1), \quad \mathbf{R} = 0.1 \cdot \mathbf{I}_{3 \times 3} $$
Eq. 4: Cost Weights

The optimal gain matrix \(\mathbf{K}\) is obtained by solving the Continuous-Time Algebraic Riccati Equation (CARE):

$$ \mathbf{A}^T \mathbf{P} + \mathbf{P} \mathbf{A} - \mathbf{P} \mathbf{B} \mathbf{R}^{-1} \mathbf{B}^T \mathbf{P} + \mathbf{Q} = 0 $$ $$ \mathbf{K} = \mathbf{R}^{-1} \mathbf{B}^T \mathbf{P} $$
Eq. 5: CARE and LQR Gain

The control law combines LQR state feedback with APF guidance forces:

$$ \mathbf{u}_i = -\mathbf{K} \mathbf{x}_e + \frac{1}{m} \nabla_{\mathbf{r}} V(\mathbf{r}) $$
Eq. 6: Combined Control Law

3. Artificial Potential Fields (APF)

The guidance layer computes a total potential energy landscape and follows its negative gradient. The total potential is the sum of three components:

$$ V(\mathbf{r}_i) = V_{\text{target}}(\mathbf{r}_i) + V_{\text{formation}}(\mathbf{r}_i) + V_{\text{obstacle}}(\mathbf{r}_i) $$
Eq. 7: Total Potential Energy

Target Attraction

$$ V_{\text{target}} = \frac{1}{2} k_a \| \mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_{\text{target}} \|^2 $$ $$ \mathbf{F}_{\text{target}} = -k_a (\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_{\text{target}}) $$
Eq. 8: Attractive Potential and Force

Formation Consensus

$$ V_{\text{formation}} = \frac{1}{4} k_c \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \| (\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j) - (\mathbf{d}_i - \mathbf{d}_j) \|^2 $$ $$ \mathbf{F}_{\text{consensus}} = -k_c \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \left[ (\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j) - (\mathbf{d}_i - \mathbf{d}_j) \right] $$
Eq. 9: Consensus Potential and Force

Obstacle Repulsion

$$ V_{\text{obstacle}} = \sum_{k} \begin{cases} \eta \left( \frac{1}{\rho_k} - \frac{1}{\rho_0} \right)^2 & \rho_k < \rho_0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ $$ \mathbf{F}_{\text{obstacle}} = \sum_k -2\eta \left( \frac{1}{\rho_k} - \frac{1}{\rho_0} \right) \frac{1}{\rho_k^2} \hat{\mathbf{r}}_k $$
Eq. 10: Repulsive Potential and Force

where \(\rho_k = \|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{o}_k\| - r_k\) is the signed distance to obstacle \(k\), \(\rho_0 = 3.5\) m is the activation radius, and \(\eta = 30.0\) is the repulsion gain.

4. Graph Laplacian Consensus

The communication topology is defined by a spatial graph \(\mathcal{G} = (\mathcal{V}, \mathcal{E})\) where edges exist between agents within \(R_{\text{comm}} = 8\) m:

$$ [\mathbf{A}_{\mathcal{G}}]_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & \|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j\|_2 \leq R_{\text{comm}}, i \neq j \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ $$ \mathbf{L} = \mathbf{D}_{\mathcal{G}} - \mathbf{A}_{\mathcal{G}} $$
Eq. 11: Adjacency Matrix and Graph Laplacian
Algebraic Connectivity. The second smallest eigenvalue of the Laplacian, \(\lambda_2(\mathbf{L})\), measures the connectivity of the swarm graph. A strictly positive \(\lambda_2 > 0\) guarantees that the network is connected and consensus can be achieved. Our simulation maintains \(\lambda_2 = 7.0\) (fully connected topology throughout).

5. Lyapunov Stability Proof

The closed-loop system stability is verified via a Lyapunov candidate function equal to the total potential energy plus kinetic energy:

$$ \mathcal{V}(\mathbf{r}, \dot{\mathbf{r}}) = V(\mathbf{r}) + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^N m \| \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i \|^2 $$
Eq. 12: Lyapunov Candidate

The time derivative along system trajectories is:

$$ \dot{\mathcal{V}} = \sum_{i=1}^N \left( \nabla_{\mathbf{r}_i} V \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i + m \ddot{\mathbf{r}}_i \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i \right) $$ $$ = \sum_{i=1}^N \left( -\mathbf{F}_{\text{total}} \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i + (\mathbf{u}_i - b \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i + m\mathbf{d}_i) \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i \right) $$ $$ = \sum_{i=1}^N \left( -\mathbf{F}_{\text{total}} \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i + (-\mathbf{K}\mathbf{x}_e + \mathbf{F}_{\text{total}} - b\dot{\mathbf{r}}_i + m\mathbf{d}_i) \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i \right) $$ $$ = -\sum_{i=1}^N \left( \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i^T (\mathbf{K}_p + b\mathbf{I}) \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i \right) \leq 0 $$
Eq. 13: Lyapunov Derivative (negative definite)

Since \(\dot{\mathcal{V}}\) is negative definite (assuming bounded disturbances), the system is asymptotically stable about the target formation. Wind gusts appear as bounded disturbances \(d_i(t)\) and produce bounded tracking errors that decay exponentially once the gust subsides.

6. Dryden Wind Turbulence Model

Atmospheric turbulence is modeled using a first-order Gauss-Markov process that matches the Dryden power spectral density:

$$ \Phi_{uu}(\omega) = \sigma^2 \frac{2L}{\pi} \frac{1}{1 + (L\omega)^2} $$ $$ \dot{w}(t) = -\frac{1}{\tau} w(t) + \sigma \sqrt{\frac{2}{\tau}} \xi(t) $$
Eq. 14: Dryden Spectrum (longitudinal) and Shaping Filter

With turbulence intensity \(\sigma = 2.0\) m/s (light-to-moderate gusts) and correlation time \(\tau = 5.0\) s. The discrete-time implementation uses an exact exponential update:

$$ w(t + \Delta t) = e^{-\Delta t / \tau} w(t) + \sigma \sqrt{1 - e^{-2\Delta t / \tau}} \, n, \quad n \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) $$
Eq. 15: Discrete-Time Gauss-Markov Process
