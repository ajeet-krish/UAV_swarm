Methodology

1. Decentralized Consensus Algorithm

Each drone operates autonomously with no centralized coordinator. Communication is purely local: each agent shares its position and velocity with neighbors within \(R_{\text{comm}} = 8\) m. The consensus law drives the relative offsets between agents toward the desired formation geometry:

$$ \mathbf{u}_i^{\text{consensus}} = -k_c \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \left[ (\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j) - (\mathbf{d}_i - \mathbf{d}_j) \right] $$

The desired offsets \(\mathbf{d}_i\) define the formation shape. The wedge is continuously rotated in 3D using a Direction Cosine Matrix (DCM) aligned with the leader's filtered velocity vector, keeping the formation oriented into the direction of travel.

2. Velocity-Command Guidance Architecture

Instead of commanding absolute positions, the guidance layer generates a desired velocity vector for each agent. This provides smoother transient response and natural saturation behavior:

Leader Guidance

The leader computes a unit vector toward the target waypoint and scales by cruise speed:

$$ \mathbf{v}_{\text{des}} = \frac{\mathbf{r}_{\text{target}} - \mathbf{r}_{\text{leader}}}{\|\mathbf{r}_{\text{target}} - \mathbf{r}_{\text{leader}}\|} \cdot v_{\text{cruise}} $$ $$ \mathbf{F}_{\text{guidance}} = k_{\text{follow}} (\mathbf{v}_{\text{des}} - \dot{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{leader}}) $$

Follower Guidance

Each follower tracks a virtual target position relative to the leader, combined with velocity matching:

$$ \mathbf{r}_{\text{target},i} = \mathbf{r}_{\text{leader}} + \mathbf{d}_i $$ $$ \mathbf{v}_{\text{des},i} = \dot{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{leader}} + k_{\text{follow}} (\mathbf{r}_{\text{target},i} - \mathbf{r}_i) $$

3. Obstacle Geometry and Distance Functions

Sphere Obstacles (Orbital Debris)

The signed distance to a sphere is the simplest analytical distance function in 3D. The gradient is exact and points radially from the sphere center:

$$ \rho_k = \|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{o}_k\|_2 - r_k $$ $$ \hat{\mathbf{n}}_k = \frac{\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{o}_k}{\|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{o}_k\|_2} $$ $$ \nabla V_{\text{obstacle}} = \sum_k -2\eta \left( \frac{1}{\rho_k} - \frac{1}{\rho_0} \right) \frac{1}{\rho_k^2} \hat{\mathbf{n}}_k $$

4. 3D DCM Formation Rotation

As the leader climbs, dives, and turns through the debris field, the wedge offsets are rotated in 3D using a Direction Cosine Matrix (DCM) aligned with the leader's instantaneous velocity vector:

$$ \hat{\mathbf{u}}_x = \frac{\dot{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{leader}}}{\|\dot{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{leader}}\|} $$ $$ \hat{\mathbf{u}}_z = \frac{\hat{\mathbf{u}}_x \times \hat{\mathbf{k}}_{\text{ref}}}{\|\hat{\mathbf{u}}_x \times \hat{\mathbf{k}}_{\text{ref}}\|} $$ $$ \hat{\mathbf{u}}_y = \hat{\mathbf{u}}_z \times \hat{\mathbf{u}}_x $$ $$ \mathbf{R} = \begin{bmatrix} \hat{\mathbf{u}}_x & \hat{\mathbf{u}}_y & \hat{\mathbf{u}}_z \end{bmatrix}^T $$ $$ \mathbf{d}_i(t) = \mathbf{R}(t) \, \mathbf{d}_i^{\text{nominal}} $$
Eq. 11: DCM Construction and Offset Rotation

The leader's velocity is low-pass filtered (\( \alpha = 0.05 \)) to prevent wind gusts or avoidance jerks from causing formation snapping. The reference up-vector switches from +Y to +X during near-vertical climbs to maintain a well-conditioned rotation matrix.

6. Wind Gust Rejection Strategy

The Dryden wind model injects stochastic velocity perturbations independently on each axis. When a drone is displaced by wind, the consensus force from its neighbors pulls it back into formation. This demonstrates the key advantage of decentralized control: every drone acts as a virtual spring-damper for every neighbor, providing natural disturbance rejection without explicit fault detection.
