What to Watch For

Formation Convergence

Seven drones fly in a 3D DCM heading-aligned wedge. The formation error decays from an initial transient as the LQR controller converges. The wedge rotates in 3D to follow the leader's direction of travel.

Obstacle Negotiation

The debris field contains six spherical asteroids (purple wireframes). APF repulsion forces with analytical signed-distance gradients push drones away from each sphere.

Wind Gust Rejection

Dryden-model turbulence (sigma=2 m/s, tau=5s) perturbs each drone independently. The consensus force from neighboring agents pulls displaced drones back into formation.

Ghost Targets

Small wireframe spheres show the desired formation offset positions relative to the leader. The gap between a drone and its ghost visualizes the instantaneous formation error.

Metric Plots

The four charts below show the full simulation history. The formation error rises momentarily during tight obstacle dodging and decays as consensus recovers.
