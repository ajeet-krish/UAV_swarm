2D Corridor Path Planning

APF-based path planning in a constrained corridor (Y ∈ [−5, 5], X ∈ [−15, 15]) with obstacle avoidance, gain sweep analysis, Monte Carlo validation, and sensitivity characterization.

What to Watch For

APF Potential Contour

The amber-red background shows the repulsive potential field. The drone follows the negative gradient, balancing target attraction with obstacle repulsion and wall confinement.

Round-Trip Navigation

The drone traverses from start (−12, 0) to goal (12, 0) and back. Speed varies as repulsion from nearby obstacles perturbs the nominal 3 m/s cruise.

Wall Confinement

Amber dashed lines mark corridor boundaries at Y = ±5. The APF wall repulsion term keeps the drone within bounds at all times, even during tight obstacle negotiation.

Gain Sensitivity

The k_avoid parameter controls repulsion strength. The gain sweep overlay and sensitivity heatmap below show how different gain values affect trajectory shape and success rate.

Gain Sweep Overlay

Trajectories for ten values of k_avoid (0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 10). Very low gains barely react to obstacles; high gains take wide, safe arcs. The fixed layout has 15 obstacles in a heavy-density configuration.

Faceted Trajectory Comparison

Each panel shows a single gain value with the same obstacle field. Gains span from barely functional (k_avoid=0.05) through optimal (4) to over-reactive (10).

Clearance per Gain

Minimum Euclidean clearance from the drone to each obstacle over the full trajectory, per gain value. Lower gains (0.05–0.5) can pass perilously close; k_avoid=4 provides a good balance of safety and path efficiency. The red dashed line marks the 0.5m caution threshold.

Speed Profile Overlay

Instantaneous speed over time for each gain value. Low-gain trajectories barely slow down near obstacles (minimal detour), while high-gain trajectories brake through tight corridors.

APF Force Breakdown

Stacked area chart showing attractive, repulsive (obstacle), and wall forces acting on the drone during the optimal trajectory (k_avoid=4, rho0=3.5). Attraction dominates in open space, repulsion spikes near obstacles, and wall forces confine the drone to the corridor.

Monte Carlo Validation

Twenty random obstacle layouts (15 obstacles each, heavy-density with narrow gaps) were generated with stochastic radii (0.3–2.2m). The APF guidance at k_avoid=4, rho0=3.5 achieved a perfect 20/20 success rate.
